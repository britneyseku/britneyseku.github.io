About me

I'm a graduate student exploring the intricate world of animal behavior, with a focus on social behaviors and the underlying mechanisms that govern them. Some of my previous research explored the impact of social hormones on schooling behaviors in the anti-social blind cavefish. Currently, my work centers on how environmental predictability affects social behavior, learning, decision-making, and personality in zebrafish. For a deeper look into my research, feel free to download my CV (updated December 2024).

Beyond research, I'm passionate about science communication. Through my microblog, Behavior with Britney (@BehaviorwithB), I summarize the latest behavioral science findings, making them accessible and engaging for all audiences.
